# PASS 2: Challenge — What's the Strongest Argument FOR Building?

**The EU AI Act argument:** Real-time monitoring for high-risk AI becomes mandatory August 2026. That's 5 months away. Companies are scrambling. There's a compliance-deadline-driven buying window.

**Counter:** Every one of the 23 existing competitors is making the exact same pitch. Guardrails AI, Arthur AI, CalypsoAI, Aporia — they're ALL positioning against EU AI Act compliance. We'd be the 24th company making this pitch with no track record, no enterprise references, and no compliance certifications.

**The "interceptor API" angle:** Building a lightweight middleware that sits between AI agents and their outputs, applying rules before execution. Simple concept.

**Counter:** This is literally what Guardrails AI ($7.5M), Aporia ($25M), and CalypsoAI ($68M) already built. With years of head start, enterprise deployments, and case studies. Our overnight version would be a toy compared to their production-grade platforms. And enterprise buyers don't buy toys for compliance.

**The open-source angle:** Build an open-source guardrails framework and monetize hosting/enterprise features.

**Counter:** Guardrails AI is already open-source (guardrailsai on GitHub). NVIDIA NeMo Guardrails is open-source. The open-source play is taken.

**Verdict from Pass 2:** Every possible angle is covered by funded competitors. There is no gap.
