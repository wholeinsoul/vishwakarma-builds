## Problem Description

A septic system fails and the clock starts. Raw sewage in the yard. A family that can't use their own bathroom. The replacement costs $15,000 to $30,000, and the contractor they find through a neighbor's cousin shows up three days late with no references and no written estimate. Trust infrastructure exists for every other home service. For septic, it's word of mouth and hope.

## Solution

Pumpline is a marketplace where septic providers earn reputation through verified work history, transparent pricing, and reviews from neighbors who hired them. Homeowners browse providers for free and see what matters before they call: response times, photos of completed work, pricing for comparable jobs. The platform tracks maintenance schedules for every property serviced, building a service record that follows the home through ownership changes, inspections, and sales. One emergency call becomes a maintenance relationship. One maintained property becomes a data point no one else has.

## MVP Strategy

Start in one rural county. Onboard five to ten septic providers with strong local reputations by hand and ask them to invite their best customers to leave reviews and upload photos of completed work. Those existing relationships seed the platform with the trust signals that don't exist anywhere else in the industry. Ship a basic directory first. Layer in maintenance tracking once homeowners are searching and providers are responding. The playbook for onboarding one county becomes the template for the next fifty.

## Revenue Model

- Providers pay $50 to $200/month for premium listings and lead priority
- A homeowner tier at $10/month adds maintenance reminders and emergency prioritization
- The real asset builds quietly underneath: every service call logged creates a property-level maintenance record that follows the home through ownership changes, inspections, and sales
- Counties need that data for permits. Insurers need it for risk models. Real estate agents need it at closing
- The marketplace sells trust. The data underneath sells to everyone else.
