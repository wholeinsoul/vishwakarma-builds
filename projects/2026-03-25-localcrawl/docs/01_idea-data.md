# IdeaBrowser Idea of the Day — March 25, 2026

## Title
**Themed Neighborhood Crawls That Drive Foot Traffic to Local Businesses** (LocalCrawl)

## URL Slug
`neighborhood-business-crawl-builder`

## Problem Description
There's no Yelp for walking a neighborhood. No tool that routes a visitor from this coffee shop to that gallery to the vintage store around the corner. Local business districts market themselves door by door. Foot traffic clusters around whatever's findable and the rest of the strip splits the leftovers.

## Solution
LocalCrawl turns neighboring businesses into a single walkable route. Districts build themed loops: a coffee-and-bookshop crawl, a gallery trail, a Saturday food tour. Each route runs on a mobile web app with GPS and QR check-ins at each stop. The data those routes generate is the real product: which stops draw the longest visits, which themes convert to purchases, which businesses perform better next to each other.

## MVP Strategy
Map one themed route with five businesses. Offer the first month free in exchange for a QR code at the register. Print route cards and leave them at the first stop, the nearest hotel lobby, and the local tourism office. First technical build: QR check-ins that timestamp each visit. If 30% of route walkers buy something at stop three, that's the foot traffic proof a $200/month subscription needs.

## Revenue Model
- Businesses pay $200-$500/month for placement + analytics
- Visitors pay $10-$25/route
- Tourism boards and business improvement districts = institutional unlock
- Three seasons of route data gives a district a number to put in front of a grant committee
