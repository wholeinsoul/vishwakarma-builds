# IdeaBrowser Idea of the Day — March 22, 2026

## Title
**Visa Route Planner for Long-Term Travelers** (Bordermath)

## URL Slug
`visa-stacking-route-planner-3295`

## Problem Description
Long-term travel planning has a dirty secret: the visa math is harder than the trip itself. Schengen counts 90 days across 27 countries on a rolling window. Thailand resets entry limits without announcing it. Indonesia's visa-on-arrival rules change by airport. A traveler stitching together eight months across Southeast Asia is running a compliance operation whether they know it or not.

## Solution
Bordermath sequences multi-country routes around visa windows. Plug in destinations and dates. The platform calculates re-entry windows, tracks Schengen days, and flags when a planned stay would push past its legal limit. When policies change, the route updates before the traveler has to scramble. A Schengen miscalculation that would have surfaced at passport control gets flagged three weeks out. As more travelers log real entry and exit data, the policy layer gets sharper than any government database alone.

## MVP Strategy
The core logic is a sequencing problem: given these destinations, passport restrictions, and stay durations, what order keeps the traveler legal? Pull visa requirement data from public government databases and layer route optimization on top. Onboard 20-30 long-term travelers manually and watch where the tool breaks. The product earns trust the first time it catches a miscalculation someone would have missed. Start in the Schengen zone where the rules are complex enough to need the tool and documented enough to build against.

## Revenue Model
- Free for basic two-country planning
- $15/month: unlimited destinations, live policy tracking, deadline alerts
- $50/month Pro: multi-passport support, budget optimization across visa fees + flight costs
- Coworking space partnerships (Lisbon, Bali, Chiang Mai) = distribution without ad spend

## Growth Strategy
Every new regulation and border policy shift adds another reason the spreadsheet approach falls apart. Coworking space partnerships put Bordermath in front of hundreds of travelers without a dollar of ad spend.
