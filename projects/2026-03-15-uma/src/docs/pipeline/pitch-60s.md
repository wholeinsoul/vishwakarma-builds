# 60-Second Pitch — Uma

## Hook (5 sec)
"My mother couldn't read her doctor's prescription. I solved it in one WhatsApp message. Now I'm building it for 150 million Indian parents."

## Problem (10 sec)
"Every day, 100 million elderly Indians struggle to read their doctor's handwriting. They forget which pill to take when. Going back to the pharmacy is painful. Their children — living in other cities or other countries — can't help in real time. The result? 30-70% medication non-adherence, preventable hospitalizations, and families that worry constantly."

## Solution (15 sec)
"Uma is a WhatsApp-only service. Parents send a photo of their prescription. Our AI reads it and sends back a beautiful Hindi infographic — which medicine, how many, what time. Then we send daily reminders. The twist? Each reminder comes with their child's photo and voice saying 'Papa, dawai kha lo.' If they miss a dose, we alert the child instantly. No app to download. Just WhatsApp — the only app Indian parents already use."

## Traction (10 sec)
"I built the first version for my own mother. She said it's the most useful thing anyone has done for her health. India has 600 million WhatsApp users, 150 million elderly on chronic medication, and zero services doing this in WhatsApp natively. The medication adherence market is $4.5 billion globally, growing 15% annually."

## Market (5 sec)
"Our addressable market is 40 million elderly WhatsApp users on chronic medication in India, plus 32 million NRI children willing to pay premium prices to manage their parents' health remotely."

## Team (5 sec)
"I've lived this problem — both as an engineer who built the AI solution and as a son 8,000 miles from his mother. I understand the technology and the emotion."

## Ask (10 sec)
"We're bootstrapping to 10,000 subscribers at ₹199-799/month. At 90%+ gross margins, that's ₹2-5 crore ARR ($250K-$600K). First target: 1,000 subscribers in 90 days through WhatsApp-native viral referrals. We're building this starting today."
